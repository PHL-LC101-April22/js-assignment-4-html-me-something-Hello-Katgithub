🧁 My Go-To Places in Philly

1) KPOT ⭐ ⭐ ⭐ ⭐

Description: All you can eat Korean bbq and hot pot place. KPOT allows you to pick either or both. The best of both worlds. This place is perfect for spending big dinners with your friends and family. You'll have to cook your own food or have a designated person mastering the grill for the KBBQ. I gave this place a 4 star rating because there's usually a long wait (1-2 hours on weekends dinner).

Price: $29.99 per person, +$5 if you want to do both KBBQ and hotpot.

Favorite Dish: Spicy broth with udon noodles and fish roe balls.

(Picture originally from Whatnowphilly.com)

2) Pho 75 ⭐ ⭐ ⭐ ⭐ ⭐

Description:

Authentic and simple Vietnamese culture staple. There is only one item on the menu, PHO! Pho is a noodle dish usually toppped with beef and vegetable garnish. Pho 75 offers a variety of meat cuts. The service is quick and consistent. Make sure to bring cash, no cards are accepted.

Price: $10-$12

Favorite Dish:

Pho with beef brisket. I usually ask for my meat on the side and then add the beef into the bowl when it all gets served at the table. This helps prevent the meat from overcooking.

(Picture originally from Reddit.com)

3) Monk's Cafe ⭐ ⭐ ⭐ ⭐

Description: Belgium restaurant that serves many types of beer on tap. This is one of my favorite places to bring my friends when its their first time in Philly! A great walk to the place and the menu has something for everyone. They are known for their mussels in a bucket. They will not SKIMP you out on the fries.

Price: $25-$30

Favorite Dish: Ghent mussels with the burger.
